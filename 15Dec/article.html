From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

In grammar, an article is any member of a class of dedicated words that are used with noun to the identifiability of object type or itself. [vague] The category of articles constitutes a part of speech.

Articles combine with nouns to form noun phrases, and typically specify the grammatical definiteness of the noun phrase. In English, the and a (rendered as an when followed by a vowel sound) are the definite and indefinite articles respectively. Articles in many other languages also carry additional grammatical information such as gender, number, and case.

Types of article [ edit ]

Definite article [ edit ]

"Definite article" redirects here. For the comedy album, see Definite Article.

A definite article is an article that marks a definite noun phrase. Definite articles, such as the English the, are used to refer to a particular member of a group.

For example:

  1. Give me the book.
  2. Give me a book.

The definite article can also be used in English to indicate a specific class among other classes:

The cabbage white butterfly lays its eggs on members of the Brassica genus.
